About Me

I am a Doctoral Researcher at the Technical University of Denmark (DTU), specializing in the intersection of Photonics and Artificial Intelligence[cite: 3, 51]. My research focuses on developing Machine Learning-based frequency comb generation in Micro-Ring Resonators[cite: 4, 51].

Prior to joining DTU, I served as a Junior Research Fellow at DRDO CHESS, where I developed Reinforcement Learning control frameworks for Tiled Aperture Coherent Beam Combining systems—technology with critical applications in defense and high-power laser systems[cite: 52, 53, 55]. I hold an M.S. in Electrical Engineering from IIT Madras (CGPA: 9.13/10.0), where my thesis work successfully validated phase control algorithms for large-scale laser arrays[cite: 5, 10, 14, 32]. I am deeply interested in leveraging Deep Reinforcement Learning to solve complex physical and scientific challenges[cite: 16].

Research Highlights

Masters Thesis: RL for Laser Beam Combining

Developed and deployed a robust phase control algorithm using Reinforcement Learning for Tiled Aperture Coherent Beam Combining Systems[cite: 14]. This work addressed the control of phases across hundreds of laser beams to achieve high-intensity outputs for defense and nuclear fusion applications[cite: 15].
